<Heading type="article" level="2">
  Implementation
</Heading>
<Paragraph className="mb-4">
  Many components in our library come with built-in shadow states. Exercise
  caution when choosing to replace or remove these shadows, as they are an
  integral part of the component's design. All shadow rules are defined using
  TailwindCSS and ships with the standard shadow rules.
</Paragraph>

{' '}

<Heading type="article" level="3">
  Dos and Don'ts
</Heading>
<DoAndDont
  direction="horizontal"
  doChildren={<p>Use shadows consistently across similar UI elements.</p>}
  dontChildren={<p>Mix multiple shadow styles arbitrarily.</p>}
/>

{' '}

<DoAndDont
  direction="horizontal"
  doChildren={
    <p>
      Apply subtle transitions to shadow properties for hover or active states.
    </p>
  }
  dontChildren={
    <p>Use abrupt changes in shadows that can be jarring to the user.</p>
  }
/>

{' '}

<DoAndDont
  direction="horizontal"
  doChildren={
    <p>Use shadows to enhance, but not replace, other visual cues.</p>
  }
  dontChildren={
    <p>Rely solely on shadows to convey the meaning or status of elements.</p>
  }
/>

{' '}

<DoAndDont
  direction="horizontal"
  doChildren={
    <p>
      Keep performance in mind and opt for simpler shadow effects where
      possible.
    </p>
  }
  dontChildren={
    <p>
      Use overly complex shadows that could slow down rendering or look
      unnatural.
    </p>
  }
/>
